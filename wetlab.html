Wet Lab

COUPLING roGFP READOUT TO EXPRESSION

Overview

The goal of wet lab was to use roGFP-Orp1 as a reporter and as a switch for graded control over downstream gene expression. Wet lab created a green-light sensitive expression cassette requires many components. These components include: a PcpcG2 promoter; ccaS and ccaR for light-sensitive signal transduction; Ho1 and pcyA1 for phycocyanobilin synthesis (required for transcription factor activity); a reporter gene (for proof of concept) - lacZ, antibiotic selection, fluorescence, luciferase; and E. coli strain JT2.

Green-Sensitive Promoter

The green-sensitive promoter requires both the transcription factor and the promoter to function. Everything is brought together into the same system, which requires Orp1-roGFP, the transcription factor for the promoter, and a plasmid with that promoter and our protein of interest.

LOV Domain

Wet lab took advantage of a protein domain called LOV (light-oxygen-voltage), which is usually incorporated as a fusion protein to binds a protein of interest to inhibit function, but dissociates when exposed to blue light. Our product can give off blue light to dissociate the LOV domain.
